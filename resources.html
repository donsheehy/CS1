Free Online Resources for Introductory CS

Organized to match the topic areas in topics.md. Resources marked ★ are especially recommended for this course.

Complete Courses (cover many topics at once)

★ Harvard CS50x — The gold standard free intro CS course. Covers abstraction, algorithms, data structures, programming in C/Python/SQL/JavaScript, and more. Free with certificate. 40+ hours; videos, problem sets, and projects. Excellent production quality; great for self-paced or supplemental use.

★ Harvard CS50P: Introduction to Programming with Python — Python-only version of CS50, more focused than CS50x. Ideal supplement for sections 4–8 (programming, data structures, algorithms, recursion).

★ MIT OpenCourseWare 6.100L — The actual MIT intro course (updated 2022). Full lecture videos, problem sets, and exams. Covers programming through data structures and computational thinking. Rigorous; well-matched to this course's level.

MIT OpenCourseWare 6.0001 — Older version of the MIT intro course (2016). Still excellent; slightly more accessible than 6.100L.

Khan Academy Computing — Free, browser-based. Covers AP CS Principles topics: digital information, internet, algorithms, and programming. Good for review and lighter introductory content. Less rigorous than the above but highly accessible.

Free Textbooks

★ How to Think Like a Computer Scientist (Interactive Edition) — Free, browser-based interactive Python textbook via Runestone Academy. Code runs inline. Covers topics 4–9 (programming fundamentals through OOP). Excellent for self-study and assignments.

★ Problem Solving with Algorithms and Data Structures using Python — Runestone Academy, 3rd ed. The best free resource for topics 5–6 and 8 (data structures, algorithms, recursion). Includes interactive code, visualizations, and exercises.

Automate the Boring Stuff with Python — Al Sweigart, free under Creative Commons. Very practical; motivating for students because every chapter produces something useful. Good complement for section 4 (programming fundamentals) and section 11 (data/files).

Think Python, 3rd ed. — Allen Downey, free online. Clear and concise intro to Python with good exercises. Overlaps with Runestone's thinkcspy but slightly more concise.

Video Series (YouTube)

★ Crash Course Computer Science — 40 episodes, ~10 min each. Covers the full sweep of CS: history, hardware, Boolean logic, networking, programming, algorithms, OS, AI, and ethics. Hosted by Carrie Anne Philbin. Ideal for topics 1–3, 10, 13–16. Not a substitute for coding practice, but excellent conceptual grounding.

★ Crash Course AI — 20 episodes. Covers ML, neural networks, NLP, computer vision, ethics. Good match for topic 16.

3Blue1Brown: Essence of Linear Algebra / Neural Networks — Stunning visual explanations. Useful for topic 17 (math foundations) and AI/ML. Not Python-focused; use selectively.

Topic-Specific Resources

Topic 1 – History of Computing

  • Crash Course Computer Science episodes 1–2 (early computing) and 16–20 (software, internet)
  • Computer History Museum online exhibits — free

Topics 2 & 7 – Computer Organization, Abstraction

  • nand2tetris — Free course + book. Build a computer from logic gates up through assembler and OS. Exceptional for topic 2 (architecture) and topic 7 (abstraction). Project-friendly.
  • Crash Course CS episodes 3–8 (Boolean logic, ALU, registers, memory, CPU)

Topic 3 – Networks and the Internet

  • Khan Academy: The Internet — free, short interactive lessons; covers IP, DNS, HTTP, encryption
  • Crash Course CS episode 28 (computer networks) and 29 (internet)
  • CS50x Week 8 (networking and web)

Topics 4–9 – Programming (Python), Data Structures, Algorithms, Recursion, OOP

  • MIT 6.100L (primary)
  • How to Think Like a Computer Scientist (Runestone) — interactive exercises
  • Problem Solving with Algorithms (Runestone) — data structures and algorithms
  • Automate the Boring Stuff — practical projects

Topic 10 – Computational Thinking

  • CS Unplugged — free activities teaching CS concepts without computers; good for early weeks

Topic 11 – Data and Information

  • Kaggle Learn: Python & Pandas — free, browser-based notebooks. Good intro to data analysis with Python.
  • Automate the Boring Stuff, chapters on CSV and file handling

Topic 12 – Visual and Creative Computing

  • CMU CS Academy — free Python curriculum centered on graphics. Engaging for beginners; good for projects.
  • The Coding Train (YouTube) — creative coding projects (Processing/p5.js, some Python). Inspiring for generative art and simulations.

Topic 13 – Software Development Practices

  • MIT: The Missing Semester of Your CS Education — free. Covers Git, shell, debugging, editors. Excellent for topic 13.
  • GitHub Skills — free, interactive Git/GitHub tutorials

Topic 14 – Theory of Computation

  • Crash Course CS episode 40 (the limits of computation)
  • Intro to Theoretical Computer Science (Udacity) — free; covers computability and complexity

Topic 15 – Ethics, Society, and Impacts

  • Crash Course AI episode 18 (Algorithmic Bias and Fairness)
  • Exploring Computer Science (ECS) — free curriculum with strong social impact focus
  • Ethics in Technology (Harvard CS50 AI) — CS50's AI course includes ethics; free

Topic 16 – Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

  • CS50 AI — Harvard's free intro AI course (Python). Covers search, knowledge, ML, neural networks
  • Crash Course AI (full playlist, above)
  • fast.ai: Practical Deep Learning — free; more advanced, but accessible final-week material
  • Google ML Crash Course — free; good conceptual intro to supervised learning

Topic 17 – Mathematical Foundations

  • Khan Academy: Discrete Math — covers logic, sets, combinations
  • Discrete Mathematics: An Open Introduction — free textbook; useful reference for proofs, graphs, combinatorics
  • 3Blue1Brown for intuition on linear algebra and probability
