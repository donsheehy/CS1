CS1: Computer Science Foundations

This course builds a foundation in computer science. It is taught at the level of a high-quality university course, but it is targeted at home-schoolers in grades 7-12.

Catalog Description

This course provides a foundation in computing and coding.
On the computing side, it will include

  • the use of computing tools such as the terminal and git,
  • the architecture of computers and the web,
  • the history of computing, and
  • applications.

On the coding side, it will include

  • problem solving,
  • algorithms,
  • programming principles, and
  • Python

At the end of the course, students will have clear mental models of how computers work and how problems are solved in code. These models will allow them to understand and explain why computers or programs behave as they do. They will be comfortable with complexity. They will be able to write programs from scratch. They will demonstrate this knowledge by writing and sharing substantial programs in Python for a variety of applications.

About the Professor

Don Sheehy studied computer science at Princeton University and later completed his PhD. in the Computer Science Department at Carnegie Mellon University. From 2013-2019, he was a professor at the University of Connecticut. Since 2019, he is a tenured Associate Professor at North Carolina State University. His main area of research is theoretical computer science, specifically working on the design of algorithms and data structures in computational geometry. He has published over 50 peer-reviewed papers. This course is independent of his teaching and research roles at NCSU.
